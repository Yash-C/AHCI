Personal Reflection

In this reflective report, I will be writing about how we worked as a team, what my responsibilities were, and things I have learned through this project. I am a Bachelor of Computer and Information Science student, majoring in software development. I donâ€™t have any experience in working in the industry.

At the start of this project, we decided to use a mouse for the device, and we changed a couple of times after that because we could not decide on what to use. It was challenging to come up with a question for a while but we were able to come up with one during one of the meetings after having a discussion with Robert. However, we worked well together as a team, we had weekly meetings where we discussed on different usability testing methods, and how we will be conducting it. After everything was confirmed, we had one practice test and went straight into inviting participants and conducting tests. During these tests, we all knew what we were doing and worked coherently.

In this research project, I was responsible for note-taking during the testing, doing research and writing a literature review. At first, I researched on usability testing in general, but in one of our meetings exploratory testing was mentioned so that is what I have moved on to. However, after we have gotten the confirmation on the device to be tested on, we managed to narrow it down to qualitative user testing, and I started to do research on that.

During the tests, it surprised me how people feel differently about the same feature in a product. For example, there is a slider feature in the website that is displaying different combinations of a timetable as a user drag the slider handle. While I felt its purpose and function were fairly obvious with the prompt displayed above, six out of ten participants did not realise that it was a slider and were confused as to what it does. This made me realize the importance of usability testing on a product before releasing it. There are multiple tests that need to be completed, such as acceptance testing which is done by a fellow member of the product development team. However, through this project, I have learned that to truly test how real-life users will interact with the product and to determine any major design flaws, some form of usability tests must be conducted. This experience will keep reminding me that usability studies are an important part of the development process, because at the end of the day, it is the users that will be using the product.
