Personal Reflection

In the era of technology where we are spending more time using computers and other technologies for our everyday lifestyle, this was a great breath of fresh air to be understanding how we as humans interact with computers and how they interact with us back helping you learn and develop better systems in the future. I am a final year student studying a bachelor’s in computer science with a major in software development here at AUT. Previously I have been part of many different group projects Such as building an IOS AR game, making a website where users can purchase plants and working at a Wholesale Company developing a website where I collaborate with editors and graphic designers. This has given me plenty of knowledge and experience on which key features we need to be aware of to ensure that nothing causes delay to the project overall.

During this study I was interested to work in a new group over a topic that I hadn’t put much thought to personally. We as a group struggled initially to come up with a good study as different parties thought differently when we did end up coming up with an idea, we further had more issues regarding how to measure the idea. Once decided upon a usability test it was difficult to find out how can we track and discover something was bad or good. However, as we have all worked in groups before we were able to come up with facts and methods that would best suit this study and came up with the method of conducting the study during our weekly meetings. We as a group did well to meet up and communicate over Facebook messenger to keep each other in the loop and put all the information on Google drive so that all members could access it when needed.

My initial task was to come up with a post interview survey that can be used to further find out more details about the subject’s experience using the website. I initially had come up with 11 questions which then were reduced to 10 after a team meeting as the 10 questions seemed to provide the most information needed for this project. I was later tasked with gathering data from the video recordings so that way the subjects can be measured using time. While the team analysed the data, I spend some time creating a website that could be used to showcase our project. Lastly, I worked alongside Matt created the information to be displayed onto the methodology page.

I found out the importance of usability tests on a system as it showcased how different subjects had different issues and provided feedback on areas of improvement. In the end the project helped me understand why and how to conduct a thorough usability test and how it can aid in the process of developing a software or system.
